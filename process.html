Design Process & Workshops

How I turn chaos into clarity.

A behind-the-scenes look at the methods, workshops, and frameworks I use to move from fuzzy problem to polished product — every time.

The Five-Phase Process

01

Discover & Understand

Start with questions, not answers. Exploratory research, competitive analysis, user interviews, and surveying establish the full landscape before any design decisions are made.

Discovery Research Competitive Analysis User Interviews Surveying
02

Synthesis & Design

Raw research becomes actionable insight. Problem statements, design principles, personas, and research artifacts give the team a shared foundation to design from — so decisions aren't made in a vacuum.

Problem Statements Design Principles Personas Research Artifacts
03

Concept Design

Divergent thinking before convergent thinking. Style tiles, mood boards, sketching, and parallel concept development explore the possibility space before narrowing in. The goal is options, not answers — yet.

Style Tiles Mood Boards Sketching Divergent Concepts
04

Prototype & Build

Low, mid, and high fidelity prototypes bring concepts to life — giving stakeholders something tangible and giving users something to react to. A prototype is a question. Testing is how you answer it.

Lo-Fi Wireframes Mid-Fi Designs Hi-Fi Prototypes Site Architecture
05

Test & Iterate

Designs are hypotheses until tested. Usability testing, iteration cycles, and final delivery ensure that what ships is what users actually need — not what we assumed they needed in phase one.

Usability Testing Iterations Style Guide Final Deliverables

Workshop Frameworks

Five types of workshops, one goal — getting the right people aligned on the right things before design gets expensive to change.

Discovery Workshops

Create alignment on overall goals, scope, and milestones. The best time to have this conversation is before a single wireframe exists.

Design Workshops

Foster collaborative culture, generate a wide range of ideas, and refine chosen directions. The best designs come from rooms where everyone has a voice — not just the loudest one.

Empathy Workshops

Shift mindsets to user-first. Share prior user knowledge and convince stakeholders of necessary changes through the lens of real user needs. Data helps. Stories close the deal.

Critique Workshops

Objectively review existing designs against criteria, principles, and user needs — without ego, with intention. The goal isn't to defend the work. It's to make it better.

Prioritization Workshops

Align stakeholders around business goals, bring in users to determine feature value, and manage scope creep after design has begun. Keeps the roadmap honest.
