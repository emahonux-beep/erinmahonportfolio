I'm a product designer with a background in psychology and information science — which means I've always been more interested in how people think than how things look.

That combination is what makes me obsessive about clarity, hierarchy, and the kind of structure that makes complex systems feel navigable.

Design Philosophy

Design isn't about making things look good.

It's about making things work better for people — reliably, at scale, in systems that are inherently messy.

Clarity through structure

Information hierarchy isn't a visual exercise — it's a cognitive one. When structure is right, users don't have to think. They just find. I obsess over how content is organized so every screen tells a coherent story from top to bottom.

Design as an iterative process

The first version is a hypothesis. I treat every design artifact as a test — staying attached to outcomes, not outputs. Iteration isn't a sign of failure. It's the process working correctly.

Data as a thinking tool

Qualitative research tells me what and why. Quantitative data tells me how much. Together, they give me the full picture to make confident decisions — and prove the impact of those decisions to the people who need to see it.

Organized chaos → intentional clarity

Healthcare systems, government portals, connected device ecosystems — inherently messy domains. My job is to find the threads of logic inside the chaos and surface them as clear, trustworthy experiences.

Leadership and mentorship

Design gets better when designers get better. I invest in mentorship, critique culture, and frameworks that make the whole team stronger. Teaching people how to think, not just what to do.

Working With Me

The question I ask on every project, at every stage.

What truly needs to be known right now — and what can live a few steps deeper?

Great design isn't about adding more. It's about removing what doesn't matter so the right things can surface.

At Express Scripts, I led the creation of our design file structure through collaborative workshops and built a Confluence hub to track changes, share resources, and create transparency across projects — now used widely to keep cross-functional partners aligned. Structure isn't just something I design for users. It's how I work.
