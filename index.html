How I Work

Research Led

Not as a phase — as infrastructure. The best design decisions are the ones built on understanding that compounds over time, not a single round of testing before launch.

Data Informed

Analytics don't validate design — they pressure-test it. I use behavioral data to find where experience breaks down, then design to close the gap.

Systems Thinking

Healthcare is never just one screen. I design for scale — components, patterns, and shared foundations that hold together across teams, products, and lines of business.

Recognition

2024 Cigna CEO Standout Award

One of five teams selected for the quarter from nominations across the entire Cigna organization — call center, clinical, HR, engineering, design, and everyone in between. Thousands of employees. A handful of winners.

Digital teams don't often win this. The competition isn't other product orgs — it's nurses, coordinators, and teams whose impact is immediate and visible. Getting a design team into that group meant the work had to be measurable in terms the whole organization understood.

The work was the Savings Advisor experience — a three-year prescription cost optimization project that became something more than a feature. Conversion improved 320%. Positive member sentiment rose 10 points.

Config 2025

What actually stuck.

Figma's annual conference in San Francisco. I sat on my notes for a few weeks before writing anything — I wanted to know what actually stuck, not just what felt exciting in the moment.

A few things did. Inga Hampton gave real, specific permission to let the process be messy — 1,000 unnamed layers, sticky notes that only make sense to you, a flow state that takes 40 minutes to reach. All of it valid. The tidy version is the deliverable. The messy version is where the thinking happens. Gabriel Valdivia made the case for sharing earlier than feels comfortable — communicable isn't the same as good, and sometimes the feedback you need can only come from the rough version.

The session I didn't see coming: the production designer behind Severance's set. A reminder that every object and surface in an environment communicates something — and that the emotional register of an interface is a design decision, not an afterthought.

What I'm actually doing differently: earlier stakeholder shares, AI for divergent thinking before I've committed to a direction, and less apologizing for how my Figma files look mid-process.
