Spotivity

Redesigning a youth activity platform to drive re-engagement — without overhauling the existing framework. Constraint-led design that proved one well-placed CTA change could move the whole experience.

Certification Flatiron School Team of 4

Role

UX Designer (Team of 4)

Timeline

4 Weeks

Type

Certification Project

Challenge

Three things were quietly killing engagement.

Users didn't understand the Personality Insight Tool — they were answering questions without knowing why, and the results didn't connect visibly to anything they could act on.

Users felt stuck at "Enroll Now" — a label that read as permanent, financial, and irreversible to teenagers who just wanted to explore.

The Explore page didn't match how teens actually think about finding activities — location-based clustering made sense architecturally, not behaviorally.

The Insight

Simply changing the label shifted the entire problem space.

Research narrowed the scope fast. We started looking for missing activity details from agencies. We ended up focused on something smaller and more tractable: the friction at enrollment.

The insight: teens didn't need more information to enroll. They needed less commitment to express interest. By replacing "Enroll Now" with "I'm Interested!" we removed the barrier without requiring agencies to provide more data.

Disengaged teen users need accessible and relevant activity details to make informed decisions about enrollment.

Usability Testing

Four tasks. Clear results.

Task 1 — Personality Insight Tool: When instructions were clear and results explicitly linked to recommended activities, teens understood the tool's value immediately.

Task 2 — Apply Filters: With reorganized and expanded categories, teens felt more in control of the search process.

Task 3 — Explore via Map: Teens value the map when it's integrated seamlessly with their filtered preferences.

Task 4 — "I'm Interested!" CTA: Teens viewed the updated CTA as significantly less commitment than "Enroll Now." Lower commitment meant more confidence clicking. More confidence meant higher conversion.

Reflection

When you can't rebuild everything, you find the one thing that actually matters.

Clients often have a vision that exceeds what's feasible. The constraint here — don't overhaul the existing framework — turned out not to be a limitation. It was the discipline that made the solution possible.

The "Enroll Now" label was doing more damage than any missing feature. It wasn't a design failure. It was a framing failure — and framing is a design problem. When clarity increases, engagement follows.
