Challenge

Every option at once is the same as no structure at all.

The Savings Advisor experience launched as a single-page model. Every eligible savings opportunity was surfaced simultaneously — generic alternatives, mail-order options, formulary changes, and more — without any hierarchy or sequencing to help members understand which action mattered most.

Members would review the options, hesitate at the complexity, and exit without taking action. The savings were real. The structure made acting on them feel harder than it was. Conversion was low. Sentiment reflected the frustration.

Three Phases

From single-page to flexible switching platform.

Phase one established the baseline and identified the core friction: members needed sequencing, not simultaneity. The design moved from showing everything at once to prioritizing the highest-value opportunity and letting members explore from there.

Phase two introduced a switching model — members could move between savings types with clear context about what they were evaluating and why it mattered to them specifically.

Phase three refined the experience based on behavioral analytics and usability testing, tightening the copy, simplifying the comparison interface, and improving the post-selection confirmation flow.

What the Data Showed

Analytics pressure-tested every decision.

Conversion tracking showed exactly where members were dropping off in each phase — and which design changes moved the needle. Sentiment tracking ran alongside conversion to ensure we weren't optimizing for clicks at the expense of member confidence.

The three-year engagement produced compounding returns: each phase built on a clearer understanding of member behavior than the last. By phase three, design decisions were being made with enough accumulated evidence that the direction was rarely in doubt.

Reflection

The work that measurably moved health outcomes at scale.

This project became the basis for the 2024 Cigna CEO Standout Award — one of five teams selected for the quarter from nominations across the entire organization. Digital teams don't often win this. Getting a design team into that group meant the work had to be measurable in terms the whole organization understood.

Over three phases, the design moved from a single-page model to a flexible switching platform. Conversion improved 320%. Positive member sentiment rose 10 points. Work that measurably moved health outcomes at scale — recognized at the level of the whole company, not just the product org.
