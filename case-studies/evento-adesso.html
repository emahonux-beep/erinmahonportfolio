Evento Adesso

Remote work normalized overnight. Event hosts suddenly needed a way to create, manage, and monitor events they couldn't physically be present for. Evento Adesso was designed not just to replicate in-person events online — but to measure and intentionally increase engagement.

Certification Flatiron School Team of 2

Role

Co-designer (Team of 2)

Timeline

6 Weeks

Type

Certification Project

Challenge

Hosts presented into a void, with no reliable signal that their content was landing.

Virtual events suffered from passive attendance. Existing platforms were built for execution — the mechanics of starting and stopping a stream — rather than around the host's real need: knowing whether anyone was actually engaged.

Covid-19 accelerated the problem. A behavior that was already growing became an overnight requirement for millions of hosts who had never designed a remote event before.

The Problem Pivot

From replication to measurement.

Early research pointed toward replication — making remote events feel more like in-person ones. Further ideation pushed the team to a more useful frame: rather than trying to recreate the physical experience, design for something digital-native.

A platform that doesn't just host events but measures whether they're working. Measurement became the differentiator. Not just more features — a feedback loop that didn't exist anywhere else.

It's very much a two-way street. It's important to engage people in some kind of way so that they're having an experience and contributing as much as they're receiving. Otherwise, people tune out.

— Dr. Mike Campbell

The Final Design

Four tasks. Built from the best of three directions.

Task 1 — Signing Up: Seamless sign-in with clear onboarding. An event planning service for socially connected users.

Task 2 — Viewing My Channel: Channel page showing video content with engagement statistics — likes, saves, shares, subscriber count, and comments.

Task 3 — Viewing My Dashboard: A unified view of event statistics, post-event submissions, and messages.

Task 4 — Creating an Event: An event editor with layered customization — music, time, ratio, interactions, backgrounds, templates, and screen alerts.

Reflection

Designing for engagement measurement gave the platform a purpose no existing tool had claimed.

The most useful reframe in this project was moving from replication to measurement. Trying to make remote events feel like in-person events is a losing battle — the medium is different, the behavior is different, and the tools need to be different too.

The constraint of six weeks and a team of two forced every decision to be deliberate. There was no room for features that didn't directly serve the problem statement. That discipline produced a more focused product than a longer timeline might have.
